Planetary Details

Learn more about each planet in our Solar System, including their mass, size, distance from the sun, moons, and more.

Mercury

The smallest planet, closest to the Sun. No moons.

Orbit: 88 Earth days

Moons: 0

Temperature: -173°C to 427°C

Venus

Earth's twin in size but with a scorching atmosphere.

Orbit: 225 Earth days

Moons: 0

Temperature: 462°C (average)

Earth

The only known planet to harbor life, with vast oceans and diverse ecosystems.

Orbit: 365.25 Earth days

Moons: 1

Temperature: -88°C to 58°C

Mars

The red planet with dusty landscapes and the tallest volcano in the solar system.

Orbit: 687 Earth days

Moons: 2

Temperature: -125°C to 20°C

Jupiter

The largest planet, a gas giant known for its Great Red Spot storm.

Orbit: 11.9 Earth years

Moons: 95

Temperature: -108°C (average)

Saturn

Famous for its stunning ring system made of ice and rock particles.

Orbit: 29.5 Earth years

Moons: 146

Temperature: -139°C (average)

Uranus

An icy blue planet that spins on its side with a faint ring system.

Orbit: 84 Earth years

Moons: 27

Temperature: -224°C (average)

Neptune

The farthest known planet, with supersonic winds and a deep blue hue.

Orbit: 165 Earth years

Moons: 14

Temperature: -218°C (average)
